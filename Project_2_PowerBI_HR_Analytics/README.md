## Problem Statement
The objective of this project is to analyze employee data to identify attrition patterns and key workforce trends using Power BI.

## Tools Used
- Power BI
- Excel

## Business Questions
- What is the overall attrition rate?

- Which departments and job roles have the highest attrition?

- How does attrition vary by age, income, and gender?

- Which employee groups should be prioritized for retention strategies?

## Key Insights
- The overall employee attrition rate is 16%, indicating a notable level of workforce turnover.
- Research & Development experiences the highest attrition count, followed by the Sales department.
- Laboratory Technician and Sales Executive roles show the highest attrition among job roles.
- Employees who left the organization contribute significantly less to total monthly income compared to retained employees.
- Attrition is higher among male employees (63.29%) compared to female employees (36.71%).

## Recommendations
- Prioritize retention strategies in Research & Development and Sales departments where attrition is highest.
- Conduct role-specific engagement and career development programs for Laboratory Technicians and Sales Executives.
- Review compensation, workload, and growth opportunities for employees in high-attrition roles.
- Implement targeted retention initiatives for employee groups with higher attrition risk.
- Continuously monitor attrition metrics using this dashboard to support data-driven HR decisions.
